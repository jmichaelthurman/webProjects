Ernest Hemingway

Tragic Brilliance

Ernest Hemingway was a 20th century American journalist, fiction author and adventurer known for an economical and terse diction. He authored ten novels, ten short story collections and five non-fiction works.

Though the pinnacle of Hemngway's career was his 1954 Nobel Prize for Literature, he is most remembered as an outdoor adventurer and the patriarch of a creative family cursed by depression.

Hemingway's most well-known published works inlcude

  • "Indian Camp" (1924)
  • The Sun Also Rises (1926)
  • A Farewell to Arms (1929)
  • Death in the Afternoon (1932)
  • Green Hills of Africa (1935)
  • For Whom the Bell Tolls (1940)
  • The Old Man and the Sea (1951)

    • Hemingway died in July, 1961, of a self-inflicted gunshot wound after a protracted battle with illnesses mental and physical.
